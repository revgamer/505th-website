GENERAL HANDBOOK

Version 2.0

This handbook contains information that will be referenced by administrators when addressing rules and regulations. You will find expected unit etiquette and infraction punishments in this handbook. You will be expected to know this information in order not to unknowingly break unit regulations. These rules may be updated at any time. Rule violations may result in client restrictions or bans.

TABLE OF CONTENTS

01 COMMUNITY GUIDELINES

"Men, keep your eyes downrange, fingers on the triggers, and we all come home in one piece. Am I right, Marines?"

Global Guidelines

The following apply to any instances you are with the 505th.

  1. You cannot be in multiple Halo units with the 505th.
  2. Any form of harassment, racism, bullying, sexism, implied or otherwise, is strictly prohibited.
  3. No arguing or fighting. If you have a disagreement, handle it like adults with conversation. If that cannot happen, contact your NCO.
  4. Respect the rank of senior soldiers. Follow orders even if you disagree with them. You are encouraged to bring up grievances after the operation in a constructive way.
  5. If there is an NCO abusing their power, please report them to an Officer immediately and a case will be opened.
  6. Privates are not allowed to join 505th Combat Operations until they have passed Boot Camp.
  7. The 505th TeamSpeak and Arma server are for members ONLY.

Discord

When in our Discord server you must know the rules listed in the discord and abide by them. Additionally, Discord TOS will be strictly enforced as well within our server.

TeamSpeak

When in our Teamspeak you will abide by the rules following:

  1. All content shared on our TeamSpeak server must adhere to our Global guidelines.
  2. Soundboarding is prohibited.
  3. Malicious links are prohibited.
  4. Advertising is prohibited.
  5. Microphone spam is prohibited.
  6. Entering unauthorized channels without the express permission of the channel admin is prohibited.

In-Game

The following is a list of the in-game rules you will be expected to abide by.

  1. Follow the instructions of your Squad leader and/or mission Commander.
  2. No trolling or griefing.
  3. No political or religious discussions or references including names.
  4. Do not destroy the spirit of the Operation by commenting on Zeusing issues, poor performance, and other things that could break immersion during an operation.
  5. Do not discharge or fire weapons on base unless you are at an appropriate location to do so.
  6. Do not discharge or fire weapons to frighten or scare civilians unless given expressed orders.
  7. Do not harm or kill any civilians. (Some extreme exceptions can bypass this rule but only for story purposes)
  8. Do not lone wolf or separate from your squad without express orders.
  9. SIDE channel is used for important zeus issues only. All other communications are to be done in the group channel or direct.

02 IN-GAME ETIQUETTE

Asset Use

What is an asset?

An asset is any equipment that the unit may use on the battlefield. This may include mortars, tanks, ships, or planes.

  • Not everyone is allowed to use them and certification is typically required beforehand.
  • If you are unsure whether you are authorized to use an asset, ask an NCO first to avoid receiving an infraction.

Usage Rules:

  • Artillery, including mortars, must remain silent until called to fire by infantry.
  • Support assets—with the exception of tanks—must not engage the enemy on their own accord.
  • Assets in immediate danger may defend themselves, but must not initiate engagements.
  • All personnel using assets must have a working microphone for proper coordination.
  • Yellow Tow Carts are reserved for Air Force use only. Unauthorized use is strictly prohibited.

Fratricide

What is fratricide?

It is the killing of one's own forces. This includes destroying friendly equipment.

Fratricide will trigger a case to be opened by an NCO. An account of the incident will be created by gathering eyewitness statements.

  • If the fratricide is deemed accidental, no punishment will occur.
  • If the fratricide affects more than 5 individuals, a mark will appear on the offender's record to track repeated offenses.
  • If repeated marks appear, retraining may be required.
  • If the fratricide is deemed intentional, the offender will receive infractions, depending on the severity.

Loadouts

Upon entering an event, you'll have access to the Loadout Locker, where all approved unit loadouts are available for selection.

You are free to choose from the roles you're qualified for, but remember: The Squad Leader has final authority and may override your selection to better suit the needs of the mission.

Mission first. Team always.

Squad Play

Teamwork is the backbone of success. In the 505th, squads operate as tight-knit units where coordination, communication, and mutual support are non-negotiable.

  • Follow your squad leader's instructions at all times.
  • Watch each other's backs—your survival often depends on it.
  • Work as a team, not as individuals.

You are highly encouraged to:

  • Discuss tactics and strategies with your fireteam
  • Share gameplay tips to elevate each other
  • Organize or attend extra training sessions to strengthen unit cohesion

A strong squad is built in downtime, tested in combat, and remembered in victory.

Insubordination

Disobeying orders undermines operational effectiveness and disrupts the immersive experience we aim to create.

Orders are to be followed. If you disagree with a directive, you are encouraged to voice your concerns respectfully, but the squad leader's decision is final during the mission.

If you believe a squad leader is abusing their authority or acting inappropriately, do not escalate the situation mid-mission. Instead, report the issue to an NCO after the operation.

Event Types

Deployment (Large-Scale Operation)

Deployments are our most extensive missions, spanning entire regions and involving coordinated combined arms warfare.

Duration: Up to 3 hours Frequency: Once per month Attendance: High-priority

Operations (Mid-Scale Combat Missions)

Operations are medium-tier events hosted more frequently and are generally infantry-centric.

Duration: Variable Frequency: Weekly (Fridays/Saturdays) Focus: Tactical, squad-based gameplay

Patrols (Low-Intensity Sector Engagements)

Patrols are smaller, immersive events that emphasize roleplay, sector control, and civilian interaction.

Duration: Up to 1 hour Frequency: Any day of the week Limit: One Patrol per day

Field Trainings (FTX)

Field Training Exercises (FTX) are designed to improve unit cohesion, communication, and tactical proficiency.

Duration: Up to 2 hours Scheduling: One week in advance Host: Qualified Zeus required

Name Rules

Names must be your typical first and last names found in cultures around the world. You cannot use "usernames" for your name.

03 INFRACTIONS

Infractions are issued as disciplinary action to individuals who violate unit rules, both in-game and within community spaces.

  • Three Infractions: Upon receiving a third infraction, the member will be demoted to Private, with all ranks and specialist roles revoked.
  • Additional Infractions: Any infractions beyond this point will result in a permanent ban from the unit.

NCOs (Non-Commissioned Officers) may issue additional consequences based on the severity or context of the offense. These may include, but are not limited to:

  • Temporary suspension from operations or deployments
  • Loss of privileges
  • Mandatory retraining or probation

04 ATTENDANCE

Active participation is essential to maintaining unit cohesion and operational readiness. In the 505th, all members are expected to:

  • Attend at least three official events per month, this includes Patrols, Field Trainings, and Operations.
  • Remain reasonably active on Discord

Failure to meet these expectations may result in being placed on inactive notice. If you are unable to attend due to personal circumstances (e.g., work, school, emergencies), it is your responsibility to notify an NCO in advance to arrange an exemption or temporary leave.

Important: If you fail to communicate your absence, you will be reported AWOL after three months of no activity. Being designated AWOL will see you stripped of rank and removed from the unit.

05 ENLISTED RANKS

Pvt – Private

Requirement: Enlistment into the UNSC

Entry-level rank. Starting point for all new members.

PFC – Private First Class

Requirement: Passing Basic Training

The backbone of the core. Performs frontline duties and focuses on gaining combat and strategic experience.

LCpl – Lance Corporal

Requirement: 2 months TIS or meritorious promotion

The first step into leadership. Leads by example, mentors juniors, and takes on higher tactical roles.

Cpl – Corporal

Requirement: 4 months TIS or meritorious promotion

First noncommissioned officer (NCO) rank. Responsible for small-unit leadership, supervising juniors, enforcing standards, and mission execution. Eligible to be Squad second-in-command.

Sgt – Sergeant

Requirement: 8 months TIS, clean record, and good conduct

Leads entire squads, helps plan and execute missions, serves as liaison between junior Marines and command, and advises Platoon Commanders.

SSgt – Staff Sergeant

Requirement: 12 months TIS and passing evaluation

Staff Sergeants serve as experienced noncommissioned officers who provide critical leadership at the squad and platoon level. They are responsible for mentoring junior NCOs and enlisted personnel.

GSgt – Gunnery Sergeant

Requirement: 15 months TIS and passing evaluation

Role: Assistant Company Commander

Gunnery Sergeants are senior noncommissioned officers who serve as key leaders and tactical experts within the company.

MSgt – Master Sergeant

Requirement: 20 months TIS and selection

Role: Division Administration

Master Sergeants serve as senior enlisted advisors and administrators at the division level.

First Sergeant

Requirement: 25 months TIS and selection

Role: Assistant Battalion Commander

The First Sergeant is a senior noncommissioned officer who acts as the principal enlisted advisor to the Battalion Commander.

Master Gunnery Sergeant

Requirement: Selection

Role: Staff Leader

The Master Gunnery Sergeant is a senior enlisted leader responsible for overseeing the training, discipline, and professional development of enlisted personnel across the unit.

Sergeant Major

Requirement: Selection

Role: Assistant Division Commander

The Sergeant Major is the highest enlisted rank and serves as the principal enlisted advisor to the Division Commander.

06 OFFICER RANKS

2nd Lt – Second Lieutenant

Requirement: Selection

Role: Platoon Commander

Second Lieutenants are junior commissioned officers who lead platoons in combat and training operations.

1st Lt – First Lieutenant

Requirement: Selection

Role: Company Executive Officer (XO)

First Lieutenants serve as the Company Executive Officers, acting as the second-in-command within a company.

Captain

Requirement: Selection

Role: Company Commander (CO)

Captains serve as the commanding officers of a company, responsible for the overall leadership, training, and combat effectiveness of their unit.

Major

Requirement: Selection

Role: Battalion Executive Officer (XO)

Majors serve as the Executive Officers of battalions, assisting the Battalion Commander in managing the unit's operations, logistics, and administration.

Lt Col – Lieutenant Colonel

Requirement: Selection

Role: Battalion Commander (CO)

Lieutenant Colonels serve as commanding officers of battalions, responsible for the overall leadership, strategic planning, and operational effectiveness of their units.

Colonel

Requirement: Selection

Role: Brigade Commander (CO)

Colonels serve as commanding officers of brigades, overseeing multiple battalions and their subordinate units.

Brig Gen – Brigadier General

Requirement: Selection

Role: Division Commander (CO)

Brigadier Generals command entire divisions, overseeing multiple brigades and coordinating large-scale operations.

Maj Gen – Major General

Requirement: Selection

Role: Task Force Commander (CO)

Major Generals lead task forces composed of multiple divisions or specialized units.
